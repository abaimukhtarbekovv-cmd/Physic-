12. Boltzmann Distribution and Mean Free Path Definition/Main Principle:



Boltzmann Distribution Law — Gives the probability of a particle having energy E at temperature T: P(E) ∝ exp(-E/kT).



Distribution of Molecular Speeds — Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for speeds in an ideal gas.



Mean Free Path (λ) — Average distance a molecule travels between collisions.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: The Boltzmann factor exp(-E/kT) shows that higher energy states are less probable at a given T. The speed distribution shows a spread—not all molecules have the same speed. Mean free path depends on molecule size and gas density.



Relevant Formulas: Most probable speed: v_p = √(2kT/m). Mean free path: λ = 1/(√2 π d² n), where d=molecular diameter, n=number density.



Example: In a gas at room T, most molecules have speeds near v_p, but some are much faster or slower. In a vacuum, n is small, so λ is very large.
