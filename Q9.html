9. Macroscopic Description of an Ideal Gas Definition/Main Principle:



Ideal Gas — Model: point particles, no intermolecular forces, elastic collisions.



Heat (Q) — Energy transfer due to temperature difference.



Internal Energy (U) — Total microscopic kinetic + potential energy of molecules. For monatomic ideal gas: U = (3/2)nRT.



Specific Heat (c) — Heat per unit mass to raise T by 1 K: Q = mcΔT.



Latent Heat (L) — Heat per unit mass for phase change at constant T: Q = mL.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: Heat is energy in transit. Internal energy is a state function. Specific heat measures a substance's "thermal inertia." Latent heat is energy for breaking bonds during phase changes.



Relevant Formulas: PV = nRT, Q = mcΔT, Q = mL, ΔU = Q - W (First Law).



Example: Melting ice: first Q raises T to 0°C (using c_ice), then Q melts it at 0°C (using L_fusion).
