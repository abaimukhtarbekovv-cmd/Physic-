13. Electric Dipole Definition/Main Principle:



Electric Dipole — System of two equal and opposite charges (+q, -q) separated by distance d.



Electric Dipole Moment (p) — Vector p = qd (direction from -q to +q).



Torque in Electric Field — τ = p × E; aligns p with E.



Potential Energy — U = -p·E = -pE cosθ; minimum when aligned.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: A dipole is the simplest neutral system with a non-zero external field. In a uniform field, it experiences torque but no net force. Energy is lowest when aligned with the field.



Relevant Formulas: p = qd, τ = pE sinθ, U = -pE cosθ.



Example: A water molecule is a permanent dipole. In a microwave oven, the oscillating E-field torques water molecules, generating heat.
