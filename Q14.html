14. Kirchhoff’s Rules Definition/Main Principle:



Junction Rule (Charge Conservation) — Sum of currents entering a junction = sum leaving: ΣI_in = ΣI_out.



Loop Rule (Energy Conservation) — Sum of potential changes around any closed loop = 0: ΣΔV = 0.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: The junction rule states charge cannot accumulate at a point. The loop rule follows from the conservative nature of the electrostatic field: a charge moving around a loop returns to the same potential.



Relevant Formulas: For a loop: Σε = ΣIR (accounting for signs: +ε when traversing from - to + terminal, -IR when traversing in current direction).



Example: Solving a two-loop circuit: assign currents, write junction equation(s), write independent loop equations, solve the system.
