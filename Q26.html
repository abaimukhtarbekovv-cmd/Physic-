26. Gauss’s Law in Magnetism (Note: This is identical to Topic 16, indicating a possible duplication in the list. Answer is provided again for completeness.)



Definition/Main Principle:



Gauss’s Law for Magnetism — ∮ B·dA = 0 (net magnetic flux through any closed surface is zero).



Magnetic Flux (Φ_B) — Φ_B = ∫ B·dA.



Area Vector (dA) — Perpendicular to surface.



Zero Flux Case — B parallel to surface or symmetric cancellation.



Maximum Flux Case — B uniform and perpendicular to plane of surface.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: This law implies no magnetic monopoles exist. Magnetic field lines are always closed loops; they have no beginning or end. Every line entering a volume must exit it.



Relevant Formulas: Φ_B = ∫ B·dA, ∮ B·dA = 0.



Example: For any closed surface around a bar magnet, the number of field lines entering (at S pole) equals those leaving (at N pole), yielding zero net flux.
