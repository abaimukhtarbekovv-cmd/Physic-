18. Torque and Rotational Dynamics Definition/Main Principle:



Torque (τ) — Rotational analog of force: τ = rF sinθ = r⊥F.



Moment Arm (r⊥) — Perpendicular distance from axis to force's line of action.



Newton’s 2nd Law for Rotation — Στ = Iα.



Moment of Inertia (I) — Rotational inertia: I = ∫ r² dm.



Parallel-Axis Theorem — I = I_cm + Md².



Rotational Kinetic Energy — K_rot = ½ I ω².



Work-KE Theorem (Rotation) — W = ∫ τ dθ = ΔK_rot.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: Torque depends on where and how you push. Moment of inertia depends on mass distribution. Στ = Iα is the fundamental equation of rotational dynamics.



Relevant Formulas: τ = r × F, I = Σ m_i r_i², K_rot = ½Iω². SI Units: τ [N·m], I [kg·m²].



Example: A wrench: applying force at the end gives larger torque. A solid disk accelerates faster than a hoop (same mass, radius) under the same torque due to smaller I.
