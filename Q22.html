22. Ohm’s Law: Derivation Definition/Main Principle:



Ohm’s Law — For many materials, current I is proportional to voltage V: V = IR.



Derivation — Links microscopic and macroscopic forms.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: The law states that current density J is proportional to electric field E in a conductor, with resistivity ρ as constant. The integral form V=IR follows for a uniform conductor.



Derivation & Formulas:



Differential Form: Start from electron drift velocity v_d = μE (μ=mobility). Current density J = n e v_d = n e μ E. Define conductivity σ = n e μ and resistivity ρ = 1/σ. Thus, J = σE or E = ρJ. This is Ohm's Law in differential form.



Integral Form: For a uniform conductor of length L, cross-section A, with constant E: V = E L. Current I = J A. Substitute: V = (ρJ) L = ρ (I/A) L = (ρL/A) I. Define resistance R = ρL/A. Thus, V = I R. This is Ohm's Law in integral form.



Example: A copper wire: given ρ, L, A, you can calculate R and predict I for applied V.
