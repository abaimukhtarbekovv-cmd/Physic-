25. Semiconductors Definition/Main Principle:



Intrinsic Semiconductor — Pure (undoped) semiconductor (e.g., pure Si). Charge carriers: thermally generated electron-hole pairs.



Extrinsic Semiconductor — Doped with impurities.



n-type: Doped with donor atoms (e.g., P in Si), excess electrons.



p-type: Doped with acceptor atoms (e.g., B in Si), excess holes.



p-n Junction — Boundary between p and n regions. Forms a depletion region and built-in potential.



Diode — Device with a single p-n junction. Types: Rectifier, Zener, LED, photodiode.



Transistor — Semiconductor device for amplification/switching. Types: Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT: NPN, PNP), Field-Effect Transistor (FET, MOSFET).



Physical Meaning & Explanation: Doping controls conductivity. A p-n junction allows current easily in one direction (forward bias) and blocks it in the other (reverse bias) – this is rectification. Transistors use a small current/voltage to control a larger one.



Bias Connection:



Forward Bias: Positive voltage to p-side, negative to n-side. Reduces depletion width, allows current.



Reverse Bias: Positive to n-side, negative to p-side. Increases depletion width, blocks current (small leakage).



Example: Silicon rectifier diode converts AC to DC. A MOSFET is the fundamental switch in computer processors.
