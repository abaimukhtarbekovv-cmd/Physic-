3. Isoprocesses Definition/Main Principle: Thermodynamic processes where one state variable (P, V, T) is held constant for an ideal gas.



Isochoric (V = const)



Isobaric (P = const)



Isothermal (T = const)



Adiabatic (Q = 0, no heat exchange)



Physical Meaning & Explanation: These are idealized models to study gas behavior. They show how P, V, T interrelate when one is fixed. The corresponding laws are derived from the ideal gas law PV = nRT.



Relevant Formulas & Graphs:



Isochoric (Charles' Law): P/T = const. Graph: A vertical line on P-V diagram; a straight line through origin on P-T diagram.



Isobaric (Gay-Lussac's Law): V/T = const. Graph: A horizontal line on P-V diagram; a straight line through origin on V-T diagram.



Isothermal (Boyle's Law): PV = const. Graph: A hyperbola on P-V diagram.



Adiabatic: PV^γ = const, where γ = Cp/Cv. Graph: A steeper curve than an isotherm on P-V diagram.



Example: A sealed, rigid container heated (isochoric): pressure rises. A piston under constant weight (isobaric): volume expands with heating.
