16. Definition of Magnetic Flux Definition/Main Principle:



Magnetic Flux (Φ_B) — Measure of magnetic field lines passing through a surface: Φ_B = ∫ B·dA.



Area Vector (dA) — Vector perpendicular to surface element, magnitude = area.



Zero Flux — When B is parallel to the surface or net lines in = lines out.



Maximum Flux — When B is uniform and perpendicular to the plane of the surface: Φ_B = B*A.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: Flux quantifies the "amount" of magnetic field penetrating a surface. It is crucial for Faraday's Law of Induction. The dot product means only the perpendicular component of B contributes.



Relevant Formulas: Φ_B = ∫ B·dA = ∫ B cosθ dA.



Example: A loop in a uniform B-field: flux is max when plane is perpendicular to B, zero when parallel. For a closed surface, net Φ_B is always zero (Gauss's law for magnetism).
