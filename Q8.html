8. Work Done by a Spring Force Definition/Main Principle:



Hooke’s Law — F_spring = -kx (restoring force proportional to displacement).



Spring Constant (k) — Stiffness [N/m].



Restoring Force — A force that always points toward equilibrium.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: The spring force is a conservative, varying force. Work done against it stores elastic potential energy (U = ½kx²). The negative sign indicates the force opposes displacement.



Relevant Formulas: W_spring = ∫ (-kx) dx = ½kx_i² - ½kx_f².



Diagram/Example: Block-spring system: A horizontal spring attached to a block. Graph of F vs. x is a straight line through origin with slope -k. Work to compress from 0 to -A is the area of the triangle: ½kA².
