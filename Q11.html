11. Definition of Capacitance Definition/Main Principle:



Capacitance (C) — Ability to store charge per unit voltage: C = Q/V.



Charging/Discharging — Processes of storing/removing charge on a capacitor.



Capacitance of an Isolated Sphere — C = 4πε₀R.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: A capacitor stores separated charge, creating an electric field and storing energy (U = ½CV²). Capacitance depends on geometry and dielectric material. Charging requires work against the field.



Relevant Formulas: C = Q/ΔV. Parallel-plate: C = ε₀A/d. SI Unit: Farad (F) = C/V.



Example: A camera flash capacitor charges slowly from a battery, then discharges rapidly through the bulb, producing a bright flash.
