19. Collisions: Elastic and Inelastic Definition/Main Principle:



Collision — An interaction where objects exchange forces and momenta over a short time.



Elastic Collision — Both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved.



Inelastic Collision — Momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is not (converted to heat, sound, deformation).



Total Mechanical Energy — Sum of kinetic and potential energies. Conserved only in elastic collisions and conservative systems.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: Momentum conservation is universal for isolated systems. Kinetic energy conservation is a special case (idealized) with no energy loss to non-mechanical forms. Inelastic collisions maximize energy conversion.



Relevant Formulas: Momentum conservation: m1v1i + m2v2i = m1v1f + m2v2f. For 1D elastic: v1i - v2i = -(v1f - v2f).



Example: Two billiard balls (nearly elastic). A car crash (highly inelastic). A bouncing ball loses KE each bounce (inelastic with ground).
