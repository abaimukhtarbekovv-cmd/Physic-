23. Electric Field & Superposition Principle Definition/Main Principle:



Electric Field (E) — Force per unit charge: E = F/q [N/C or V/m].



Superposition Principle — The total electric field at a point is the vector sum of fields from all individual charges.



Electric Current — Rate of charge flow: I = dQ/dt. Types: Direct (DC) constant in direction/magnitude; Alternating (AC) periodically reverses direction; Quasi-stationary slowly varying AC.



Physical Meaning & Explanation: The electric field is a vector field that exists in space due to charges. Superposition is a fundamental property of linear electromagnetic fields.



Relevant Formulas: Field of point charge: E = k q/r² * r‌. Superposition: E_total = E₁ + E₂ + ....
